Amayangri sits deep within the ancient alleyways of Boudhanath — one of the holiest Buddhist stupas in the world — yet opens its east-facing windows to the full sweep of the Langtang Himalayan range. Forest in the middle ground, snow peaks beyond.

Designed as a single-storey retreat on 14 × 20 feet of sacred ground, every element is chosen for stillness: stone walls, carved wood, morning light, the distant sound of monastery bells.

The Amayangri Philosophy

⌘
Modernly Traditional

Newari carved wood, stone walls, and pitched roof — refined with clean lines and minimal ornament. Ancient craft, contemporary spirit.

◈
East Light

Every major window faces east. The Langtang peaks catch dawn before you wake. Morning is an event here, not a routine.

⬡
Sacred Proximity

Minutes on foot from Boudhanath Stupa. The monastery bells, the circumambulating pilgrims, the smell of juniper smoke — all just beyond the gate.

Deep in the alleyways of Boudhanath

Amayangri is tucked within the sacred ring of Boudhanath Stupa — the holiest Tibetan Buddhist site outside Tibet, and a UNESCO World Heritage site. The stupa's white dome and golden spire are visible from the garden gate.

Behind the retreat, a forest buffer creates the middle ground. On clear mornings, the Langtang range — including Langtang Lirung at 7,227m — fills the eastern sky.
